Clean-Up-The-Fridge Rice

Notes

The night that I take out the trash is clean-up-the-fridge night. Sometimes I simply eat the leftovers that are reaching the end of their lifespan, and sometimes I cook them into a new dish. I’ve had many interesting clean-up-the-fridge dinners. When I have vegetables and meat to use up, but no starch, this is a good option.

Makes

About 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup uncooked rice, washed (I prefer jasmine rice for this recipe)
  • 1/4 cup cooked meat, cut into small pieces if necessary
  • 1/4 cup cooked or quick-cooking vegetables, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 2 tsp spices (I often use 1 tsp of turmeric and 1 tsp of another spice)
  • Water or broth, enough to cover rice by about 1 cm

Directions

  1. In a pot over medium heat, melt the butter and add the garlic and onion. Cook the alliums until the onion is translucent.
  2. Add rice and spices. Cook until the rice is dry and toasted and the spices are fragrant.
  3. Add your meat and vegetables and stir, then add the cooking liquid. Cover, bring to a boil, and turn the heat down to low.
  4. Cook until all the liquid is absorbed. Let rest for a few minutes and fluff the rice before serving.
