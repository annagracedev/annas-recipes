Classic Meatloaf

Notes

Meatloaf is a great dinner. I adapted mine significantly after cutting gluten because the recipe I learned used a packet of onion soup mix for seasoning, which contains things I can’t tolerate. I typically need to use more gluten-free breadcrumbs in recipes than I did regular breadcrumbs.

The first time I ate meatloaf, it was covered with bacon instead of ketchup sauce. While I’m a fan of bacon, I actually prefer meatloaf without it.

For this recipe, garlic and onion powder produce a much more consistent texture than minced fresh alliums.

Makes

4 servings

Requires

  • Broiler pan; or roasting pan and parchment paper

Ingredients

  • 500g 80-85% lean ground beef
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup gluten-free breadcrumbs
  • 60ml milk
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp thyme
  • 1 Tbsp meatloaf sauce (see below)

Sauce

  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp molasses

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350F.
  2. In a large bowl, add all of the meatloaf ingredients and mix well.
  3. Shape the mixture into a loaf and place it on top of a vented broiler pan. (You can also use a roasting pan lined with parchment paper; using a broiler drains the juices as it cooks)
  4. Bake uncovered for 50 minutes.
  5. Spread the sauce over the meatloaf, then return to the oven and bake an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the internal temperature reads 160F or the juices run clear after inserting a knife into the thickest part.
