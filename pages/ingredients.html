Ingredients

I try to cook with ingredients I can source locally, and there are a few considerations I have for my diet. I’m glad that the state of gluten-free food has improved so drastically from what it was when my mom developed Celiac disease.

Water

I use filtered water for everything in my kitchen. Tap water bothers my digestion. I tested this at multiple locations across the country and I’ve found it to be consistently true. A carbon water filter seems to be sufficient in removing whatever causes it. Maybe it’s chlorine.

Flour

I’ve used King Arthur gluten-free all-purpose flour mix as a straight replacement for wheat flour in almost every recipe I make. In my opinion, of two commonly available gluten-free flours in my area, it’s the superior choice for baked goods, while Bob’s Red Mill gluten-free mix is superior for absorbing fat in a roux. However, it’s not a significant enough difference for me to go out of my way to choose one or the other. Better results can be obtained by making custom gluten-free flour mixes for different recipes, but frankly, I do not have enough space or time in my life for that. I think that in most cases, a pre-made mix is about 80% as good, and techniques and seasoning are more important to obtaining a good result than the specific flour replacement used.

Soy Sauce

Most soy sauce contains wheat, so if you are cooking gluten-free food you must be careful to buy gluten-free soy sauce.

Mirin

Mirin contains quite a bit of sugar. There’s really no substitute for mirin. I seem to tolerate sugar fine in small doses, so I cook with mirin without worrying about it.

Milk

I use lactose-free whole milk for all my cooking. Thankfully, I tolerate many other forms of dairy well. I have never noticed a difference between using milk or lactose-free milk in any recipe, so I don’t normally specify it.
