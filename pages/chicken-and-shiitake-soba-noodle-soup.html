Chicken and Shiitake Soba Noodle Soup

Notes

Most soba noodles contain about 10% wheat flour to improve the texture, so they are not safe for gluten-free people. I have only found Eden Organics to make soba with 100% buckwheat. I haven’t attempted making fresh soba with buckwheat flour yet.

Another method of cooking this is to cook the chicken and mushrooms with soy sauce and mirin, then add broth to deglaze. After my first time making it that way I altered it; I think the chicken absorbs enough flavor from the soup.

Makes

1 serving

Ingredients

  • 250ml shiitake dashi
  • 2-3 rehydrated shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 100g chicken thighs, cut into small pieces
  • Oil
  • 2 oz 100% buckwheat soba noodles
  • Spring onion, thinly sliced
  • Soft-boiled egg, optional

Directions

  1. To make shiitake dashi, rehydrate sun-dried shiitake mushrooms in 500ml water for several hours or overnight. Save the remaining dashi for another recipe.
  2. Prepare the chicken thighs by trimming and cutting them first into strips with the grain, then into cubes against the grain.
  3. Cook the chicken thighs with a small amount of oil in a pot over medium heat until they are cooked through and no longer pink.
  4. Add the dashi, shiitake, soy sauce, and mirin. Bring to a boil for a few minutes to combine the flavors and boil off the alcohol in mirin.
  5. In another pot, boil the soba noodles for about 2 minutes less than package directions. Drain well.
  6. Place the noodles in the bottom of a serving bowl and pour the hot soup over them. Garnish with spring onion. Sometimes I cut a soft-boiled egg in half and place it on top.
