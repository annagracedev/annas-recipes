Posca

Notes

Posca, the drink of Roman soldiers, simply vinegar, water, and (probably) salt. On hot days it’s rather refreshing, somewhat like drinking lemonade or shrub. I don’t rely on it for all my hydration needs due to the low potassium and magnesium, but it does seem an improvement over drinking plain water. Slightly acidic drinks also tend to settle my stomach, so posca has an advantage over other forms of hydration there.

I’ve tried various types of vinegars in this drink. Red wine vinegar is slightly sweet and with a rounded acidic taste that stays relatively consistent. Apple cider vinegar is sweet and rather sharp. Rice vinegar is mild up front but has an extremely acidic aftertaste. Finally, balsamic vinegar is smoky with molasses-like flavor and hardly tastes acidic at all. I would probably award posca made with balsamic vinegar the most drinkable posca, red wine and apple cider vinegars coming in at basically a tie for your fruit-forward posca, and rice vinegar ranking dead last in posca palatability.
