Maple Peanut Truffles

Notes

After I had mentioned offhandedly that I would bring some chocolates to a small party, I panicked internally because unsweetened chocolate is not to the tastes of most people. So instead of making chocolate truffles with ganache as planned, I grabbed a jar of peanut butter and made something like my version of peanut butter cups. Have you noticed that peanut butter becomes more viscous when mixed when a sweetener like honey or maple syrup? This filling needed to be mixed with cornstarch to hold up well, but it was a good starting point. I kept these truffles in the fridge right up until serving. I do not think anyone noticed that there was no sugar in the chocolate coating itself...

Makes

1-2 dozen

Requires

  • Double boiler
  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper
  • Freezer space

Ingredients

  • 1 16 oz jar natural peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste (optional)
  • 2-3 Tbsp cornstarch (depending on the consistency of your peanut butter)
  • 4 oz baking chocolate (plus more, if needed)
  • Sea salt for garnish

Directions

  1. Combine peanut butter, maple syrup, vanilla, and cornstarch in a small bowl and mix thoroughly.
  2. Using a spoon (I used a 2 tsp measuring spoon), form the filling into balls (it made about 20). Place on a baking sheet covered with parchment paper and freeze for at least 30 minutes, until very cold.
  3. Melt the chocolate in a double boiler and transfer it into a small bowl to make the next step easier. (I did not temper my coating because I was feeling lazy. Temper it with a small amount of coconut oil if you like.)
  4. Using a large metal spoon, transfer one truffle at a time into the melted chocolate, turn it to coat completely, then transfer it back onto the baking sheet. Sprinkle a generous pinch of sea salt across the top.
  5. Return the baking sheet to the freezer and the chocolate to the double boiler to refreeze and reheat them as needed throughout this process.
  6. After the chocolate coating has completely set, these truffles can be stored in the fridge.
